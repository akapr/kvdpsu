Pittsburgh Restaurant Microreviews

Pete Su
Roger Rosner

This page lists restaurants that we know and either love or hate in Pittsburgh. The opinions here are our own, and try to be as honest and brief as possible. The listing is alphabetical. Maybe later when it is larger, we'll list by category as well.

61C Cafe - Murray Ave, at the bus stop

Coffee isn't great, but the place is wonderful.

Atria's - Warrendale, North side, other places

Steaks, burgers, general american food, good service, great with kids.

Bangkok Balconey - Squill, Forbes ave.

Good Thai food.

Baum Vivant - Baum Blvd.

High End Spanish/French food. I like it. I know people who don't.

Bravo - Everywhere

Mediocre pasta and such. Offensively expensive.

Cafe at the Frick (Clayton) - North of the Frick Museum

Great desserts, soup, sandwiches, lunch, sunday tea. Make reservations.

Cafe Zinho - Waterfront

Seems interesting at first, until you realize it isn't.

Casbah - In Shadyside on Highland Ave.

Reasonably good mediterranean-ish food. Too often more expensive than it is worth.

Chaya - Murray Ave, halfway down the hill.

Good Sushi and other Japanese food. Nice hot pots. Get the live scallop if they have it.

Chopsticks - Squirrel Hill

Stay Away.

Chopstick Inn - Mcyntire Square, off Mcknight Rd, North Hills.

Biggest Chinese menu that I know of. Lots of excellent and more unusual dishes. Try the Pork Belly. Don't get takeout.

Coffee Tree - Squill, Forbes Ave, other places

Brewed coffee is consistently excellent. Espresso is uneven. Good hot chocolate.

Eat 'n Park - Everywhere

Almost always open. Great with kids.

Enrico Biscotti - 20th St. and Penn, Strip.

Great biscotti, cookies, and other delights.

Fat Heads - South Side, Carson and 18th or 19th.

Good wings, lots of beer, huge sandwiches that don't suck much. Very loud.

Il Piccolo Forno - 21st and Penn, Strip.

Antonio's Italian bakery. Great pastas and salads for lunch. Stupendous breads, cakes, and pastry.

Kaya - Strip

Sometimes it's great, sometimes it sucks.

Kazansky's - Murray Ave, down the hill

Reasonably enjoyable deli stuff. Nice maztoh ball soup. Uniquely dumpy atmosphere.

La Cucina Flegrea - Murray Ave, halfway down the hill.

Best Italian in Pittsburgh. Go on Wednesdays for Risotto Night.

La Prima Espresso - 21st and Penn, Strip

The best coffee in Pittsburgh, period. Don't even bother going anywhere else.

Le Pommier - Carson St. South Side

Bistro style French. Very good, but a bit uneven. Get the Creme Brulee.

Lidia's - Strip

Good Italian.

Mad Mex - Oakland, North Hills, Mt. Leb

Enjoyable faux-Mex. Lots of beer and drinks. Loud.

Milky Way - Murray Ave

Vegatarian and Kosher. Good pizza and falafel.

New Dumpling House - Murray Ave

Chinese and sushi. Stay away.

Orient Kitchen - Oakland, Baum Blvd.

Good general Chinese. Not as good as other favorites.

Original Hot Dog Shop ("The O") - Oakland, CMU

Transcendent hot dogs. More fries than a human can consume. Dirty, greasy, possibly dangerous.

Pierogies Plus - Mckee's Rocks

Hand made Pierogies. Awesome.

P.F. Chiang's - Waterfront

Offensively bad Chinese.

Primanti Brothers - Strip, Oakland.

Those wierd-ass sandwiches with fries and slaw on them.

Regina Margherita - Bellevue, Lawrenceville

Great pizza. Expensive.

Sharp Edge - East Liberty, near Whole Foods

Good beer, bad food.

Spice Island Tea House - Oakland.

This place used to be stupendous. Now it is not.

Square Cafe - Braddock Ave, Regent Square

Good breakfast and lunch. No nonsense.

Tasty - In Shadyside on Highland Ave in the plaza just before the bridge.

Some excellent home style Cantonese. Order off of the "Hong Kong" menu only.

Taco Loco - Carson and 17th. South Side.

Real mexian in Pittsburgh. Great tacos and other dishes.

Tessaro's - Bloomfield, Liberty Ave

Local bar. Stupendous burgers. Probably the best in the city. Can be slow.

Tram's - Bloomfield

Good Vietnamese food. Pho, noodle bowls, some "special dishes".

Udipi Cafe - Old Willima Penn highway, Monroeville

Probably won't get better South Indian near here. Lots of traffic from the Temple. Good Dosa.

Umi - Shadyside, Ellsworth

High end Japanese. Very expensive, very good. Unclear if it's worth it. The fixed price tasting menu (Omakase) is nice.

Uncle Sam's - Oakland, Squirrel Hill

Good cheesesteaks. Some gripe about the bread and/or the fries. The one in Oakland is better.

Vivo - Main St. in Bellevue.

Italian-ish. Very expensive. Very excellent. No credit cards.

Zaw's - Murray Ave, down the hill.

Good takeout in Squirell Hill.
